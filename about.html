ABOUT YELLOW BASKET
San Pedro 4023 • Low Bandwidth Dreams

WHAT IS THIS?

Yellow Basket is a satirical project by Franco Mamaril that critiques the endless, often absurd world of online shopping. Today’s platforms — Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop — promise convenience far beyond the simplicity of the old sari-sari store. But with that convenience comes a new ecosystem of problems: scams disguised as discounts, algorithm-driven price manipulation, compulsive shopping habits, and mountains of plastic waste delivered straight to our doors.

THE CONCEPT

Yellow Basket exaggerates these realities by imagining an online store from a parallel future — San Pedro 4023 — where low signal, weak devices, and unstable memory turn online commerce into something dreamlike, glitchy, and strange. The project mirrors how digital marketplaces overproduce not just goods, but desires, trash, and distractions, all wrapped in orange flash-sale banners and countdown timers.

WHY SAN PEDRO 4023?

Because the city is always buffering between past and future. Between the Laguna Lake mist and the LED billboards. Between tricycle terminals and algorithmic prediction. Yellow Basket exaggerates this blur — turning everyday Laguna artifacts into speculative items from a time that hasn’t arrived yet, but already feels nostalgic.

THIS IS AN ART PROJECT

This website is not a real shop. No items will be delivered. The checkout is a metaphor for things we almost got, almost became, almost remembered. It is an experiment in internet art, satire, and place-based memory using low-tech aesthetics as a cultural language. It is both a critique and a love letter: an exploration of how Filipinos navigate digital capitalism with humor, resourcefulness, and a tendency to laugh at the chaos, even as the Add to Cart culture consumes us in return.

ABOUT THE ARTIST

Created by a multimedia artist from Laguna who grew up between weak Wi-Fi, cheap CD-Rs, Laguna Lake shoreline walks, and the strange quiet of early-morning San Pedro. Yellow Basket is built as a love letter to glitch, to hometowns, and to the humor of trying to function in low-signal conditions.
